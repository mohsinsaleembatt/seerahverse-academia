Course Objectives

Foundational Knowledge & Problem-Solving

  • Master Quantum Mechanics & General Relativity conceptually and mathematically.
  • Solve advanced physics problems up to 12th grade and beyond.
  • Understand everyday phenomena with deep, detailed explanations.

Critical Analysis & Research Skills

  • Develop intuition for the limitations of current physical theories.
  • Read, critique, and analyze original research papers from advanced physics journals.
  • Explore open questions in physics and methods to address them.

Alternative Theories & Modern Developments

  • Study alternative physics theories (e.g., Roger Penrose, Stuart Hameroff, Leonard Susskind).
  • Examine how AI is reshaping physical theories and its future implications.

Alternative Theories & Modern Developments

  • Learn to write original research papers in Fundamental Physics.
  • Understand the peer-review process and how to get published.
  • Gain a philosophical appreciation of modern physics.

Course Timing and Details

  • 1 class per week.
  • 60 minutes per class.
  • Each class will include two parts: Theoretical Discussion and Problem Solving.
  • Theoretical discussion includes lecture on new concepts, open discussion, QnA and reading excepts from Research papers and textbooks.
  • All reading material and content will be shared by the faculty.
  • Problem solving sessions will be highly interactive and students will be asked to present their solutions to the class.
  • Participation in class activities will be mandatory.
