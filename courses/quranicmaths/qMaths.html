Course Objectives

Master

  • Foundational to advanced STEM topics: Algebra, geometry, calculus, statistics, and quantitative aptitude.

Analyze

  • Islamic contributions: Study works of Al-Khwarizmi, Al-Biruni, and Omar Khayyam, and their impact on modern mathematics.

Apply

  • Sacred geometry: Explore the Golden Ratio, symmetry, and geometric patterns in Islamic art and architecture.

Develop

  • Problem-solving skills: Through hands-on projects, real-world applications, and research on Fields Medal-winning advancements.

Complete

  • A capstone project: Integrate Islamic mathematical heritage with modern STEM (e.g., algorithmic design inspired by Al-Khwarizmi).

Course Outcomes

  • Dual expertise in modern STEM and classical Islamic mathematics.
  • Critical thinking for careers in engineering, data science, or academia.
  • Appreciation for Islamic intellectual heritage and its global impact.
  • Portfolio-ready projects demonstrating interdisciplinary innovation.
